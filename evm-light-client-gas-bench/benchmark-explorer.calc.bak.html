EVM Light Client Gas Benchmark Explorer

What it costs to verify a CometBFT light-client commit on Ethereum L1, why today’s ed25519 + per-signer vote-byte signing makes that impractical, and which alternative is actually viable.

Overview

CometBFT validators sign ed25519 over per-signer canonical-vote bytes (each carries the signer’s own timestamp). Solidity has no ed25519 precompile, so verifying current commits on Ethereum L1 is not viable at quorum scale. The recommended path is secp256k1eth: validators sign secp256k1 instead, and the verifier runs the EVM-native ecrecover precompile per signer. No protocol change needed beyond the signing curve.

BLS aggregation (BLS-A, B, C) is cheaper per verify but requires one shared precommit message for the committed block, a CometBFT protocol change that isn’t on the table. Keeping per-signer messages with BLS (BLS-D) costs N+1 EIP-2537 pairings: gas grows linearly with validator count and reaches block-budget scale fast. Membership proofs (ICS23/IAVL) are priced separately under Reference.
